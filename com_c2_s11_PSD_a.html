Power Spectral Density#

Power Spectral Density of a Digitally Modulated Signal with Memory#

We analyze a bandpass modulated signal \( v(t) \) through its equivalent complex-valued, lowpass signal \( v_{lp}(t) \):

\[ v_{lp}(t) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} s_{lp}(t - nT; \vec{I}_n) \]

  • \( s_{lp}(t; \vec{I}_n) \) represents one of \( M \) possible lowpass signals based on the information sequence \( \vec{I}_n \).

  • The process \( \vec{I}_n = (\ldots, I_{n-2}, I_{n-1}, I_{n}) \) is stationary.

The goal is to determine the power spectral density (PSD) of \( v(t) \).

Autocorrelation of \( v_{lp}(t) \)#

The autocorrelation function is given by:

\[ R_{v_{lp}}(t + \tau, t) = \mathbb{E}[v_{lp}(t + \tau)v_{lp}^*(t)] \]
\[ = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \mathbb{E}\left[s_{lp}(t + \tau - nT; \vec{I}_n)s_{lp}^*(t - mT; \vec{I}_m)\right] \]

Averaged Autocorrelation#

Since \( v_{lp}(t) \) is a cyclostationary process, we average \( R_{v_{lp}}(t + \tau, t) \) over one period \( T \).

The averaged autocorrelation function \( \bar{R}_{v_{lp}}(\tau) \) is:

\[ \bar{R}_{v_{lp}}(\tau) = \frac{1}{T} \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \int_0^T \mathbb{E}[s_{lp}(t + \tau - mT - kT; \vec{I}_{m+k})s_{lp}^*(t - mT; \vec{I}_m)]\,dt \]

After a variable change \( u = t - mT \):

\[ = \frac{1}{T} \sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} \sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty} \int_{-mT}^{(m-1)T} \mathbb{E}[s_{lp}(u + \tau - kT; \vec{I}_k)s_{lp}^*(u; \vec{I}_0)]\,du \]

Definition of \( g_k(\tau) \)#

The function \( g_k(\tau) \) is defined as:

\[ g_k(\tau) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \mathbb{E}\left[ s_{lp}(t + \tau; \vec{I}_k)s_{lp}^*(t; \vec{I}_0)\right] dt \]

which represents the expected autocorrelation between pulses separated by \( k \) symbol periods.

Averaged Autocorrelation in Terms of \( g_k(\tau) \)

Next, the autocorrelation function can be decomposed into a sum of time-shifted versions of \( g_k(\tau) \) as:

\[ \bar{R}_{v_{lp}}(\tau) = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} g_k(\tau - kT) \]

Power Spectral Density (PSD) of \( v_{lp}(t) \)#

The power spectral density, \( S_{v_{lp}}(f) \), is the Fourier transform of \( \bar{R}_{v_{lp}}(\tau) \):

\[ S_{v_{lp}}(f) = \frac{1}{T} \mathcal{F}\left[\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} g_k(\tau - kT)\right] \]

By applying the Fourier transform of time-shifted signals:

\[ S_{v_{lp}}(f) = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty} G_k(f)e^{-j2\pi kfT} \]

where \( G_k(f) \) is the Fourier transform of \( g_k(\tau) \).

Expression for \( G_k(f) \)

The quantity \( G_k(f) \) is derived as:

\[ G_k(f) = \mathcal{F}\left[\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\mathbb{E}[s_{lp}(t + \tau; \vec{I}_k)s_{lp}^*(t; \vec{I}_0)]dt\right] \]

Expanding:

\[ G_k(f) = \mathbb{E}\left[\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} s_{lp}(t + \tau; \vec{I}_k)e^{-j2\pi f(t+\tau)} s_{lp}^*(t; \vec{I}_0)e^{j2\pi f t} dt d\tau\right] \]

which simplifies to:

\[ G_k(f) = \mathbb{E}[S_{lp}(f; \vec{I}_k)S_{lp}^*(f; \vec{I}_0)] \]

where \( S_{lp}(f; \vec{I}_k) \) and \( S_{lp}(f; \vec{I}_0) \) are the Fourier transforms of \( s_{lp}(t; \vec{I}_k) \) and \( s_{lp}(t; \vec{I}_0) \).

Relationship of \( G_k(f) \)

We have:

\[ G_0(f) = \mathbb{E}\left[|S_{lp}(f; \vec{I}_0)|^2\right] \]

where \( G_0(f) \) is real.

For \( k \geq 1 \), \( G_{-k}(f) = G_k^*(f) \).

We define:

\[ G'_k(f) = G_k(f) - G_0(f) \]

From this definition:

\[ G'_{-k}(f) = G'_k(f)^*, \quad G'_0(f) = 0 \]

This redefinition centers the spectral components around \( G_0(f) \) and introduces symmetry properties.

Final PSD Expression

Using these definitions, the PSD \( S_{v_{lp}}(f) \) becomes:

\[ S_{v_{lp}}(f) = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}[G_k(f) - G_0(f)]e^{-j2\pi kfT} + \frac{1}{T}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}G_0(f)e^{-j2\pi kfT} \]

Rearranging:

\[ = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}G'_k(f)e^{-j2\pi kfT} + \frac{1}{T^2}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}G_0\left(\frac{k}{T}\right)\delta\left(f - \frac{k}{T}\right) \]

Continuous and Discrete Components#

By leveraging symmetry properties and Fourier properties:

\[ \boxed{ S_{v_{lp}}(f) = S_{v_{lp}}^{(c)}(f) + S_{v_{lp}}^{(d)}(f) } \]

where:

  • Continuous Component:

\[ S_{v_{lp}}^{(c)}(f) = \frac{2}{T}\text{Re}\left[\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}G'_k(f)e^{-j2\pi kfT}\right] \]

  • Discrete Component:

\[ S_{v_{lp}}^{(d)}(f) = \frac{1}{T^2}\sum_{k=-\infty}^{\infty}G_0\left(\frac{k}{T}\right)\delta\left(f - \frac{k}{T}\right) \]
